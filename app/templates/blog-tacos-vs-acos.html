TACOS vs. ACOS: Which Metric Tells the Real Story?

There's a constant debate in the Amazon space: Should you primarily track ACOS or TACOS? While ACOS tracks the direct efficiency of an advertising campaign, TACOS (Total Advertising Cost of Sales) gives you the macroeconomic view of your entire business. Here's why you need to shift your focus.

The Organic Halo Effect

Pumping ad spend into a product often drives its organic ranking higher. If you only look at ACOS, you may see an unprofitable campaign. But if you look at TACOS, you might discover that the organic sales generated by ranking higher actually make the entire operation wildly profitable.

Scaling with Safety

You can't build a sustainable brand by starving your products of visibility. We recommend aiming for a TACOS between 8% and 12%, depending on your margin structure, rather than exclusively squeezing ACOS below 20%.
